# Universal Declaration of Human Rights

> Note: I’m not including the full official UDHR wording here. If you paste the official text you’re using, I can format it into Markdown exactly. The sections below are a structured outline with paraphrased summaries.

## Preamble (summary)

The declaration recognizes that inherent dignity and equal rights are the foundation of freedom, justice, and peace, and calls on individuals and institutions to promote these rights.

## Articles (summary)

### Article 1 — Freedom and equality

All people are born free and equal in dignity and rights, and should treat one another in a spirit of brotherhood.

### Article 2 — Non-discrimination

These rights apply to everyone, without discrimination of any kind.

### Article 3 — Life and security

Everyone has the right to life, liberty, and personal security.

### Article 4 — No slavery

No one may be held in slavery or servitude.

### Article 5 — No torture

No one may be subjected to torture or cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment or punishment.

### Article 6 — Recognition before the law

Everyone has the right to be recognized as a person before the law.

### Article 7 — Equality before the law

All are equal before the law and entitled to equal protection.

### Article 8 — Effective remedy

Everyone has the right to an effective remedy for rights violations.

### Article 9 — No arbitrary arrest

No one may be arbitrarily arrested, detained, or exiled.

### Article 10 — Fair hearing

Everyone is entitled to a fair and public hearing by an independent and impartial tribunal.

### Article 11 — Presumption of innocence

Everyone charged with a penal offence is presumed innocent until proven guilty in a public trial; no punishment without law.

### Article 12 — Privacy

No one may be subjected to arbitrary interference with privacy, family, home, or correspondence, nor attacks on honour and reputation.

### Article 13 — Freedom of movement

Everyone has the right to move within their country and to leave and return.

### Article 14 — Asylum

Everyone has the right to seek and enjoy asylum from persecution, with limits for serious non-political crimes and acts contrary to UN principles.

### Article 15 — Nationality

Everyone has the right to a nationality, and not to be arbitrarily deprived of it.

### Article 16 — Marriage and family

Adults have the right to marry and found a family with free and full consent; family is entitled to protection.

### Article 17 — Property

Everyone has the right to own property alone or with others; no arbitrary deprivation.

### Article 18 — Thought, conscience, religion

Everyone has freedom of thought, conscience, and religion, including changing religion/belief and practicing it.

### Article 19 — Expression

Everyone has freedom of opinion and expression, including seeking and sharing information across borders.

### Article 20 — Assembly and association

Everyone has the right to peaceful assembly and association; no one may be compelled to belong to an association.

### Article 21 — Participation in government

Everyone has the right to take part in government, directly or through representatives, and to equal access to public service; the people’s will is the basis of authority.

### Article 22 — Social security

Everyone has the right to social security and realization of economic, social, and cultural rights through national effort and international cooperation.

### Article 23 — Work

Everyone has the right to work, fair conditions, equal pay for equal work, and to form and join trade unions.

### Article 24 — Rest and leisure

Everyone has the right to rest and leisure, including reasonable working hours and periodic holidays with pay.

### Article 25 — Standard of living

Everyone has the right to an adequate standard of living, including food, clothing, housing, medical care, and social services; special care for motherhood and childhood.

### Article 26 — Education

Everyone has the right to education; elementary education should be free and compulsory; education should promote understanding and respect for rights.

### Article 27 — Cultural and scientific life

Everyone has the right to participate in cultural life and share in scientific advancement; creators have moral and material interests protected.

### Article 28 — Social and international order

Everyone is entitled to an order in which these rights can be fully realized.

### Article 29 — Duties and limits

Everyone has duties to the community; rights may be limited only to secure respect for others and meet the just requirements of morality, public order, and general welfare.

### Article 30 — No destruction of rights

Nothing in the declaration may be interpreted as granting any right to destroy the rights and freedoms set out in it.
