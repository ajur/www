## The Pitch

You're a reasonable teenager whose friends convinced you to throw a house party while your parents are gone for the night. As usually happens, things start to go out of control—unknown people start showing up, while litter and broken stuff pop up at an increasing rate.

As a good host, your goal is to clean up and resolve as many issues as possible before things go over the top and someone calls your parents. It's inevitable that they'll find out; the only question is: **how long can you keep the party going?**

## Project Context

This was my very first entry into any game jam, created for **Ludum Dare 50** (April 2022). The theme was *"Delay the Inevitable"*. 

I initially aimed for the "Compo" (48-hour solo) category but ended up submitting to the "Extra" format to give the game the polish it needed, including improved steering and music.

## How to Play

The game is web and mobile-friendly:
* **Movement:** Use **WASD** on keyboard or touch/click anywhere for an onscreen thumbstick.
* **Objective:** Find issues to solve (indicated by yellow and red circles). Stay inside the circle for a few seconds to resolve the issue and keep the party timer running.

## Tech Stack

* **Language:** TypeScript
* **Engine/Libraries:** PIXI.js for rendering, Matter.js for collisions, and GSAP for timeline animations.
* **Art:** Created on iPad using Procreate.
* **Audio:** Music semi-randomly composed from samples in "Launchpad DJ with Novation" (iPad); sound effects sourced from freesound.org.
