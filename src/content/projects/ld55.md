## The Pitch

Going through random items on some obscure oriental bazaar, you are determined to find an ancient lamp with a trapped Djinn. Pick up lamps, flasks and vases, rub them vigorously, and hope you don't just smash another piece of pottery. Find the lamp, rub it and summon the gienie!

## Project Context

This was my second game jam entry, created for **Ludum Dare 55** (April 2024). The theme was *"Summoning"*.

The theme was hard to tackle. I rejected the obvious "summonings" — monsters, cultists and the like (fun, but probably quite common). Then I thought about some real-life summonings. One of the better ideas was a game about a nurse's night shift in a hospital, summoned by patients, struggling to be everywhere while tracking tiredness and sleepiness. But it would be too time-consuming to pull off in a weekend.

So, as usual with "make something from a prompt" jams, as a non-native English speaker, I went to Urban Dictionary for inspiration — and found something fun. Although I kept the literal meaning, not the one from UD ;)

I initially aimed for the Compo (48-hour solo) category, but with very limited time for actual coding that weekend, I couldn't make it in time. Dropping to the Jam format was still progress compared to LD50!

## How to Play

The game is web and mobile-friendly:
* **Browse:** Click or tap items scattered across the bazaar shelves.
* **Rub:** Once you pick up a lamp or flask, rub it by dragging across it. The faster you rub, the quicker it opens — but you need to cover enough distance.
* **Objective:** There is a fixed number of lamps/flasks with exactly one containing the Djinn, mixed in with ordinary items. Types and placement are random, so you could find it on the very first try — or smash your way through the whole bazaar.
* **Enjoy the writing:** Each item comes with its own humorous description (courtesy of ChatGPT), so take your time reading them!

## Tech Stack

* **Language:** JavaScript (with a touch of TypeScript)
* **Engine/Libraries:** PIXI.js for rendering, GSAP for animations, Vite for bundling — all smashed together in a makeshift "game engine."
* **Art:** Drawn by myself, except for the background which was generated with [Leonardo AI](https://app.leonardo.ai/) and edited slightly.
* **Music:** Simple track "composed" by putting together loops in GarageBand.
* **Sounds:** Sourced from [Pixabay](https://pixabay.com/sound-effects/).
* **Texts:** Item descriptions generated with ChatGPT.
